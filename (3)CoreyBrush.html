Compliments to the Chef: Corry Brush
Take a peak into the mind of Trick Dog chef, Corry Brush. Corry shared some insight about her favorite, and least favorite, foods, her go-to spots in San Francsico, and some of the reasons she decided follow her passion of cooking.
Q: Where did you grow up?
A: I grew up in Windsor Vermont it’s actually the birthplace of Vermont. A very small town.

Q: When did you decide to become a chef?
A: I didn’t decide to work toward being a chef until I was in culinary school. I chose to go to a vocational tech school for culinary when I was 16 in high school.
Q: Was there anything that you thought you wanted to do before you started cooking?
A: I always wanted to be a marine biologist because the ocean fascinates me, I feel though that has transferred into being a chef.

Q: Where did you go to culinary school?
A: I went to culinary school at New England culinary institute, in Burlington Vermont.

Q: What brought you to San Francisco?
A: I was living in Boston Massachusetts, when I for no reason decided to move to the bay, and have been here for about 10 years now.

Q: Is there a chef you admire most? Why?
A: This question brings up a lot of emotion for me, the chef I admire most has passed, his name was Chef Chris L’hommideu. He was an amazing inspiration to me as well as a really good friend. He worked at Michael Mina restaurants as well as many Thomas Keller restaurants, I had the privilege of working with him in the Bay Area and learning an amount of knowledge that I wouldn’t exchange for anything.

Q: What is your favorite cuisine to eat?
A: My favorite cuisine to eat is hard to say...I definitely enjoy nice restaurants with amazing food such as steak and foie gras as well as amazing seafood, it in the same breathe I love a good burrito, or sandwich.
Q: What is your favorite meal to cook?
A: I will say I don’t enjoy this question, and have always said that, only because there are so many things I haven’t had the chance to cook yet. I will say I like to cook anything that I have the time to baby and make it exactly how I want it, and not be rushed.

Q: Is there a food you hate?
A: I will get flack for this I’m sure, I detest uni, I respect its place but for me I don’t enjoy it at all. Also raw onions, I love onions cooked in anything, it for my taste raw isn’t for me.

Q: Where are you currently working?
A: I currently work at Trick dog bar and restaurant.

Q: Where is your favorite place to visit in San Francisco?
A: My favorite place has to be two, I would say la taqueria in the mission on 25th, and swan oyster depot on Polk.

Q: If you could cook anyone a meal (dead or alive) who would it be and what would you cook?
A: Either Jacque Peppin or Julia child and it would be a whole roast chicken.

Q: What is your most embarrassing moment of being a chef?
A: There isn’t one moment that is the most embarrassing, it is most embarrassing for me when you don’t deliver, or make mistakes, because you always want to present the best that you can, although people always make mistakes..
